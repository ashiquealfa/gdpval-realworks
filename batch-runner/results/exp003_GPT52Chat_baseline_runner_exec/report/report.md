## Quality Analysis

Self-assessed QA confidence scores clustered between 5 and 7 for most sectors, suggesting that the model generally perceived its outputs as adequate but not highly polished. The highest average self-QA confidence appeared in Government (6.8/10) and Retail Trade (6.7/10), while Information and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services showed comparatively lower averages (5.6–5.7/10), potentially reflecting higher ambiguity or complexity in those task types.

Latency patterns indicate that Information sector tasks were significantly slower on average (45.8 seconds) despite perfect completion, suggesting heavier reasoning, longer context handling, or more complex deliverable generation. Sectors with faster average latency generally aligned with slightly higher self-QA confidence, though the correlation was weak. Deliverable file generation quality, as implied by self-QA scores, was stable but not consistently high, indicating room for refinement in formatting, completeness, or specificity.
